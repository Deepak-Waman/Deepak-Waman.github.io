## WarmWorld (BMBF, 2023-2027)

The [WarmWorld](https://www.warmworld.de/) project aims to develop and apply km-scale global ICON simulations for climate research. My contributions focus on aerosol-cloud coupling within the ICON-AES framework, using CAMS reanalysis aerosol data to drive two-moment microphysics at global scales.

**PI at KIT:** Prof. Corinna Hoose

## CHARGE -- Thunderstorm Electrification in ICON (DFG, in preparation)

CHARGE targets the development of a physically based thunderstorm electrification scheme for the ICON model. The scheme couples inductive and non-inductive charging mechanisms with a Poisson solver for the 3D electric field. Flash-rate parameterizations will be validated against observations from the LEE, DC3, and ESCAPE campaigns, with STEPS providing empirical benchmarks.

The long-term goal is to enable lightning climatology and electrification process studies in convective-permitting global ICON simulations.

## FLASHMAP -- Machine Learning for Lightning Prediction

FLASHMAP develops an XGBoost-based machine learning pipeline to predict lightning flash density. The model incorporates Saunders charge-reversal physics and is trained on observational lightning datasets. A complementary observational paper targeting ACP quantifies lightning hotspot distributions and their environmental controls.

## ORCESTRA Limited-Area Simulations

I built and tested limited-area ICON runscripts for the ORCESTRA field campaign (tropical Atlantic, 2024) and prepared LAGRANTO trajectory setups for air mass tracking. The ORCESTRA case is one of four deep convection cases evaluated in the ACP SIP manuscript.

## CloudPhysicsLab (Public Outreach)

[CloudPhysicsLab](https://github.com/Deepak-Waman/cloudphysicslab) is an open educational resource that makes atmospheric science accessible to students and educators. The platform uses interactive lessons explained by Dr. Nimbus, an animated cloud character, to introduce cloud physics and microphysics concepts.
