About

Research Focus

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Meteorology and Climate Research (IMK-TRO), Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany, working under Prof. Corinna Hoose within the BMBF-funded WarmWorld project.

My research centers on cloud microphysical processes and their representation in global atmospheric models. I develop and evaluate parameterizations for ice nucleation, secondary ice production, aerosol-cloud-radiation interactions, and thunderstorm electrification within the ICON modeling framework.

I completed my PhD at Lund University, Sweden (2019–2023), under Dr. Vaughan Phillips, where I developed the AC bin-microphysics model to study ice multiplication in mixed-phase clouds.
