## Research Focus

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the [Institute of Meteorology and Climate Research (IMK-TRO)](https://www.imk-tro.kit.edu/), Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany, working under Prof. Corinna Hoose in the BMBF-funded [WarmWorld](https://www.warmworld.de/) project.

My research centers on **cloud microphysical processes** and their representation in global atmospheric models. Specifically, I work on:

- **Secondary ice production (SIP)** mechanisms and their impacts on cloud structure, precipitation, and dynamics in deep convective systems
- **Ice nucleation** parameterizations and aerosol effects on cloud glaciation
- **Aerosol-cloud-radiation interactions** and their role in the climate system
- **Thunderstorm electrification** and lightning parameterization in convective-permitting models
- **ICON model development**: two-moment microphysics, energy conservation, and aerosol-cloud coupling within the ICON-AES framework

I completed my PhD at Lund University, Sweden (2019-2023), under Dr. Vaughan Phillips, developing the AC bin-microphysics model to study SIP and ice nucleation in mixed-phase clouds.

## Featured Research

### Secondary Ice Production in Deep Convective Clouds

Using ICON at convective-permitting resolution (1.6 km), I evaluate the role of secondary ice production processes (rime splintering, droplet shattering, collisional fragmentation) in controlling ice number concentrations and dynamics in deep convective systems. Four field campaign cases are simulated and compared against EarthCARE CPR observations.

### Thunderstorm Electrification in ICON

I am developing a self-consistent thunderstorm electrification parameterization for ICON, combining inductive and non-inductive charging mechanisms with a Poisson solver for the electric field. The scheme targets flash-rate parameterization and lightning climatology in convective-permitting global simulations.

### Aerosol-Cloud Coupling in ICON-AES

As part of WarmWorld, I have implemented a CAMS reanalysis aerosol reader within ICON-AES, enabling prognostic aerosol input to the two-moment microphysics scheme. This work supports the representation of aerosol effects on cloud microphysics in kilometer-scale global simulations.
